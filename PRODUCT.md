# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Chemistry students, instructors, and practicing scientists who need to explore equilibrium
models quickly, compare assumptions, and explain the resulting diagrams in individual study,
classroom projection, or technical review. Users range from learners who need plain-language guidance
to experts who expect editable constants, explicit conventions, and numerically reproducible output.

## Product Purpose

Equilibria Lab turns analytical-chemistry equilibrium models into interactive, inspectable workflows.
Users define the chemical system, adjust conditions, and immediately connect composition, operating
conditions, and quantitative results to a figure. Success means that a learner can understand why a
curve changes while an expert can verify exactly which model, constants, and assumptions produced it.

## Brand Personality

Rigorous, clear, and quietly confident. The product should feel academically trustworthy without
becoming austere, and approachable without simplifying away the chemistry.

## Anti-references

- Dense textbook calculators that expose equations without explaining the system or the result.
- Decorative science interfaces whose visual effects compete with plots, notation, or controls.
- Generic dashboard grids that fragment one chemical workflow into unrelated cards.
- Interfaces that hide advanced capability behind jargon, inconsistent controls, or unexplained modes.
- Citation-heavy application chrome; methodology belongs in the workflow and source detail in docs.

## Design Principles

1. Let the user define the system; presets accelerate work but never constrain the model.
2. Put the figure and its operating point first, with inputs and derived results visibly connected.
3. Reveal complexity progressively while keeping every active assumption explicit.
4. Preserve scientific notation, units, mass balance, and model limits across every interaction.
5. Use one coherent control and feedback vocabulary across all equilibrium families.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for contrast, keyboard operation, focus visibility, and semantic controls. Preserve
the complete Spanish/English interface, color-blind-safe plot distinctions, 44 px touch targets,
responsive layouts, and reduced-motion behavior. Meaning must never depend on color alone. These are
design targets — no formal accessibility audit has been run yet.
